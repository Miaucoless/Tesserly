USMLE Platform

Medical School is Hard,
Studying Shouldn't Be

Tesserly brings your notes, questions, flashcards, and schedule into one fast workspace so every study session starts with a clear next step.

Core Study Features

Import PDF, DOCX, PPTX, TXT, and Markdown into a single study library designed for flow. With folders, multi-tab note viewing, drag-and-drop uploads, and in-app viewing/editing, you can compare and refine material without breaking concentration.

What You Can Actually Do

  • Organize material by system, block, or exam target with folders and nested structure.
  • Keep multiple notes open in tabs for faster cross-referencing during active review.
  • Read and edit imported content in-app to avoid context switching mid-session.

Select Step level and question count, generate practice directly from selected notes, and review with explanation-first feedback plus wrong-answer tracking. The workflow supports both speed drills and deeper reasoning sessions.

How It Helps Day To Day

  • Turns passive reading into active retrieval practice sourced from your own material.
  • Supports timed simulation and untimed mastery depending on your study objective.
  • Keeps misses visible so weak topics are revisited deliberately.

Select one note or combine multiple sources, choose style and length, and generate a polished summary tailored to your review goal. Export to PDF for sharing/printing or save directly back to your Notes library.

High-Value Use Cases

  • Create high-yield review sheets from lecture-heavy notes before major assessments.
  • Generate concise daily revision versions without losing clinical context.
  • Save polished summaries as searchable notes for future cycles.

Create cards with custom prompts, auto cloze mode, or auto basic mode based on your study style. Save decks, run quick in-app reps, or export/copy in an Anki-friendly format for your existing spaced-repetition workflow.

What This Replaces

  • Manual card writing after each lecture or reading session.
  • Tool switching between generation, organization, and review.
  • Formatting cleanup by keeping exports clean and import-ready.
